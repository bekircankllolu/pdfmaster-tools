How to Convert PDF to Images: Save as JPG or PNG

Need to use a PDF page in a presentation, share it on social media, or embed it in a website? Converting PDF to images is the quickest solution. This guide shows you how to turn PDF pages into high-quality JPG or PNG files.

Why Convert PDF to Images?

  • Presentations -- Insert PDF pages as images into PowerPoint or Google Slides.
  • Social media -- Share document pages on Instagram, Twitter, or LinkedIn.
  • Websites -- Add document previews to blog posts or product pages.
  • Messaging -- Quickly send a page via WhatsApp or Telegram.
  • Archiving -- Organize documents in your photo gallery.
  • Printing -- Print specific pages as high-resolution images.

JPG vs PNG: Which Format to Choose?

  • JPG (JPEG) -- Smaller file size, best for photos and complex visuals. Slight quality loss due to lossy compression. Ideal for social media and web use.
  • PNG -- Lossless compression, supports transparency. Best for text-heavy documents, logos, and technical drawings. Larger file size but perfect quality.

Rule of thumb: Use JPG for photo-heavy PDFs, PNG for text and graphics.

Quality and Resolution Tips

  • For web -- 72-150 DPI is sufficient. Small file size, fast loading.
  • For presentations -- 150-200 DPI is ideal for projector quality.
  • For printing -- 300 DPI or higher for high-quality output.
  • For social media -- 150 DPI + JPG provides a good balance of speed and quality.
